Debrief

You may have noticed that it was sometimes a bit difficult to recall sentences word-for-word, even though you were recalling them almost immediately. This is totally normal!

In fact, one of the things we're investigating is how you might forget different parts of the sentence. For instance,

(1) The key to the cabinets obviously are on the table

(2) * The key are on the table

Why does (1) sound so much better than (2)? It has to do with the fact that the word between this subject noun and its verb is plural! Consider (3) below:

(3) The cabinets are wooden

...

We're looking to understand how you manipulate this wording in memory, once you've seen the whole sentence, had a second to process and maintain it, and then are made to reproduce it. Basically, how do the language processes in your own head affect the way you internalize a sentence?
